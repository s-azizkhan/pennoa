01 / The Name

A modern descendant of the pennon — the trailing banner of knights and ships.

Pennoa
/ˈpɛn.oʊ.ə/  ·  PEN-oh-ah
Etymology

From pennon (Old French penon) — the long, narrow banner trailing from a knight's lance or a ship's mast. Softens the heraldic root into a modern form ending in open vowels.

Why It Wins

Most precise semantic match — a pennon is the trailing banner the product depicts. Zero collision with notification-category codes. Literary register matches a designer-first audience without slipping into cute or corporate.

Phonosemantics

02 / Positioning

We are not building a better notification. We are reframing the alert as the product.

Competitive alternative Native macOS and Google Calendar pop-up alerts — dismissed reflexively, lost in notification fatigue.
Unique attributes A floating, cinematic airplane and banner above all applications. Non-blocking. Two-pulse cadence at T−10 and T−5. Animation is the signal.
Customer value Never miss a meeting and keep flow state intact. The reminder becomes a moment of delight rather than anxiety.
Target market Mac creatives and makers — designers, developers, writers — in deep work, allergic to corporate-style alerts.
Market category Ambient meeting reminders.

07 / Category Point of View

Why the world needs Pennoa.

01 · Observation
Notifications collapsed into noise.

Calendar alerts, Slack pings, system updates — all rendered in the same gray pill, dismissed with the same reflex.

02 · Gap
A missed meeting is not a missed update.

The OS treats them identically. The cost of dismissing one more notification is zero. So we dismiss.

03 · Ramification
Miss the meeting, or live in the calendar.

Knowledge workers either miss meetings or live inside their calendar app to compensate. Neither preserves flow.

04 · Vision
A reminder should be a visual event.

Brief enough not to break flow. Distinctive enough not to be dismissed. Designed enough to be welcomed.
